About

A personal reflection on why Intellectual Property matters to me, how trademarks became a central professional interest, and how Continuous Learning continues to shape my journey.

My Philosophy

"Intellectual Property is the oil of the 21st century."

- Mark Getty

This idea resonates with me because Intellectual Property now sits at the heart of how innovation is created, protected, and sustained.

In a knowledge-driven economy, intangible assets strongly influence business resilience, long-term competitiveness, and social progress.

Why Intellectual Property?

Intellectual Property brings together law, innovation, business, creativity, and technology in a uniquely meaningful way.

It supports entrepreneurship, protects original ideas, encourages creative work, and contributes to sustainable economic growth across industries and geographies.

Why Trademarks?

For me, trademarks are far more than legal registrations. They represent identity, reputation, trust, brand value, customer recognition, and long-term business growth.

This connection between legal structure and strategic business impact is what makes trademarks especially meaningful to me.

Professional Journey

Rather than viewing growth as a destination, I see it as an ongoing process shaped by reflection, discipline, and sustained curiosity.
